About Me

Who I Am

I'm Elias Tovar, a Computer Science student at Baylor University pursuing a degree with a concentration in Cybersecurity. I'm passionate about building secure, scalable solutions and solving complex technical challenges. My journey in technology combines hands-on experience in IT operations, security monitoring, and software development.

Currently, I work as a Security Operations Technician Intern at Texas Farm Bureau, where I've gained valuable experience in incident response, security monitoring, and log analysis. I'm constantly learning and adapting to new technologies, always seeking to understand not just how systems work, but how to make them better and more secure.

My Academic Journey

At Baylor University, I've been honored to receive the Baylor Distinction Award in 2024, which recognizes academic excellence and leadership. My coursework has provided me with a strong foundation in both theoretical concepts and practical applications of computer science.

Professional Experience

My role as a Security Operations Technician Intern at Texas Farm Bureau has given me hands-on experience with real-world cybersecurity challenges. I work with incident response, security monitoring, and log analysis to identify and mitigate potential threats. This position has taught me the importance of attention to detail, quick thinking, and effective communication in security operations.

Beyond my internship, I've worked as a Personal Shopper and Customer Service Representative at HEB, where I developed strong time management skills, achieved performance benchmarks, and learned the value of teamwork and adaptability in fast-paced environments.

Technical Skills & Interests

I'm proficient in C++ and Python, with experience building everything from game applications to automation scripts. My cloud computing experience spans both Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud, where I've worked on automating virtual machine deployments and managing cloud infrastructure.

In the realm of security, I'm experienced with Active Directory and CyberArk for identity and access management, and I'm passionate about implementing security best practices in everything I build. I believe that security shouldn't be an afterthought—it should be built into every layer of technology from the ground up.
